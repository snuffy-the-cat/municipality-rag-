# Noise Complaint Investigation

## Procedure

When a noise complaint is received, follow these steps:

1. **Initial Contact**: Respond to the complainant via phone (extension 123) or email (`noisecomplaint@municipality.gov`) to acknowledge receipt of the complaint and gather more information.
2. **Verify Information**: Confirm the address, time, and nature of the disturbance with the complainant. Ask for a description of the noise and any previous attempts made to resolve the issue.
3. **Review Municipal Code**: Familiarize yourself with Chapter 6, Article 12 of the Municipal Code regarding excessive noise.
4. **Visit Property (if necessary)**: If the complaint is valid and warrants an on-site visit, arrange for James Martinez (Code Enforcement Officer) to accompany you. Contact him via phone (extension 456) or email (`jmartinez@municipality.gov`) to schedule a joint visit.
5. **Gather Evidence**: Document any evidence of noise disturbance using photographs, videos, or audio recordings.
6. **Notify Property Owner**: Leave a notice on the property indicating that a complaint has been made and providing contact information for further action.

## Escalation Procedures

If repeated violations occur at the same location, follow these steps:

1. **Initial Warning**: Send a certified letter to the property owner (via USPS) warning them of repeated noise complaints.
2. **Second Violation**: If another complaint is received within 30 days, send a formal citation (Form EC-001) via certified mail.

* Misunderstanding or misinterpreting municipal codes can result in incorrect citations or fines.

Tips for success:

* Always document all interactions with complainants, including phone calls and emails.
* Develop relationships with local business owners and residents to better understand their needs and concerns.
* Continuously review and update your knowledge of municipal codes and procedures to ensure accuracy and effectiveness.
