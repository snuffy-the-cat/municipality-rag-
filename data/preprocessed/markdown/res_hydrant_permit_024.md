# Fire Hydrant Use Permits

## Overview

As a municipal employee responsible for managing fire hydrant use permits, your primary role is to ensure that all necessary permits are issued and accounted for in a timely manner. This responsibility involves coordinating with various stakeholders, including building owners, contractors, and emergency services.

## Procedures

1. **Monthly Review**
	* Log into UtilityConnect to review the list of pending fire hydrant use permit applications.
	* Identify any overdue permits and prioritize their resolution.
2. **Permit Application Submission**
	* Verify that all required documentation is submitted with each application (e.g., building plans, contractor licenses).
	* Use the Fire Hydrant Use Permit Form (FHUP-001) to ensure completeness and accuracy.
3. **Permit Issuance**
	* Review permit applications and issue permits via UtilityConnect, ensuring compliance with Fairview Municipal Code Section 14.2.1.
	* Notify applicants of permit approval or rejection via email.
4. **Payment Processing**
	* Process permit fees according to the Fairview Municipal Fee Schedule (FMFS-001).
5. **Record Keeping**
	* Maintain accurate records of all fire hydrant use permits issued, including dates, permit numbers, and payment status.

## Handoff Notes

I'm leaving this role to focus on other municipal priorities. As I hand over responsibility for Fire Hydrant Use Permits, please note the following:

* Review all pending applications in UtilityConnect.
* Familiarize yourself with the Fairview Municipal Code Section 14.2.1 and the Fire Hydrant Use Permit Form (FHUP-001).
* Establish a regular review schedule to ensure timely permit issuance and payment processing.

## Tips and Reminders

* Always verify contractor licenses before issuing permits.
* Be mindful of deadlines for permit applications, as outlined in Fairview Municipal Code Section 14.2.1.
* Utilize UtilityConnect for all permit-related tasks, including application submission, review, and issuance.
